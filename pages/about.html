About BTS

BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan / 방탄소년단 / Bulletproof Boy Scouts) is

a South Korean boy band formed in 2010. The group has seven

members:

  1. RM
  2. Suga
  3. J-Hope
  4. Jim
  5. V
  6. Jimin

who participate extensively in the creation of their own music,

helping to write and produce much of the material

Musical Styles

  1. Pop
  2. Hip-hop
  3. Trap
  4. R&B
  5. EDM
  6. Rock
  7. Balland

Themes Covered in the Songs

  1. Love and relationships
  2. Self-love and self-esteem
  3. Youth and personal growth
  4. Dreams and ambitions
  5. Mental health and emotions
  6. Social criticism and societal pressure
  7. Friendship and overcoming challenges

Before their debut, the members underwent a long period of intense training

in singing, dancing, and music production. Despite initial difficulties and a

small company, the group managed to debut in 2013 with the song "No More

Dream", which talks about the pressures faced by young people.


Big Hit was a nearly bankrupt company with few resources. The seven

members lived together in a cramped dormitory with only one bathroom,

faced a lack of money for promotions, and trained exhaustively, sometimes

sleeping only 2 or 3 hours a day. They dealt with public bullying, imposter

syndrome, and low visibility in their early years.


Initially, they were criticized for coming from a "small company" and for their

hip-hop concept. Throughout their career, they faced accusations of

plagiarism in lyrics, cultural controversies (such as comments about the

Korean War that caused problems in China), and common criticisms in K-pop,

such as colorism or problematic lyrics from earlier phases. Like any large

group, they also suffer from haters and excessive scrutiny for any mistake.


From an underestimated group, BTS grew organically thanks to strong fan

engagement (ARMY), the profound messages in their music, and their active

presence on social media. They went from small shows to world stadiums,

broke several Billboard records, influenced the South Korean economy

(generating billions of dollars a year), and became the first truly global K-

pop act. Today, they are seen as a phenomenon that changed the

international music industry.

Awards and Achievements

  1. Winners of numerous international awards, including
  2. Billboard Music Awards and American Music Awards
  3. First K-pop group to perform and win major categories at the Grammy Awards (with historic nominations)
  4. Global records for album and digital music sales
  5. Nominated Artist of the Year at several international awards ceremonies
  6. Recognized worldwide for their cultural impact and influence on pop music

Achievements Beyond Music

  1. Global ambassadors for social and educational campaigns
  2. Speeches and participation in international events such as the United Nations
  3. Partnerships with organizations to promote education, self-esteem, and youth support
  4. Great cultural and social influence worldwide
  5. Recognized as important representatives of South Korean culture
